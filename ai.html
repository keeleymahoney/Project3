Band History

Fleetwood Mac is a British-American rock band formed in London in 1967. The band's ever-evolving lineup has produced some of the most iconic and enduring music in the rock genre.

Originally formed as a blues-rock band, Fleetwood Mac underwent significant stylistic changes throughout the years, incorporating pop and soft rock elements into their sound.

They achieved massive success in the mid-1970s with albums like "Rumours," which remains one of the best-selling albums of all time. Check out a review of "Rumuors" here

Band Members

Fleetwood Mac has had several notable members, including Mick Fleetwood, John McVie, Christine McVie, Stevie Nicks, and Lindsey Buckingham.

The band's lineup changes over the years have contributed to their diverse musical catalog, with each member bringing unique talents and influences.

Notable periods include the Buckingham-Nicks era and the various collaborations between the core members.

Stevie Nicks and Lindsey Buckingham

Stevie Nicks and Lindsey Buckingham joined Fleetwood Mac together in 1974, bringing a new dynamic to the band with their vocal and songwriting abilities.

The romantic relationship between Nicks and Buckingham added emotional intensity to their music, particularly evident in songs like "Go Your Own Way" and "Silver Springs."

Despite their personal differences and the end of their romantic relationship, the musical partnership between Nicks and Buckingham continued to produce iconic songs throughout the years.
